🌱 Plant Fiber

Cotton

The most widely used natural fiber in the world — known for its softness, breathability, and remarkable versatility.

About

Fiber Info

Cotton fibers are seed hairs from the Gossypium plant. Each fiber is a single elongated cell that grows from the surface of the cotton seed. Under a microscope, cotton fibers appear as flat, twisted ribbons — this natural crimp gives cotton its exceptional breathability and moisture absorbency.

Cotton can absorb up to 27 times its weight in water, making it one of the most absorbent natural fibers available. It has been cultivated for over 7,000 years and remains the most popular natural fiber globally.

Process

Processing

From field to fabric, cotton goes through several stages before becoming the soft textiles we use every day.

1 🌱

Cultivation

Cotton is planted in warm climates and grows for 5–6 months before the bolls burst open.

2 🤲

Harvesting

Cotton bolls are harvested by machine or by hand, then taken to gins for processing.

3 🌀

Ginning

The gin separates cotton fibers (lint) from seeds. Lint is compressed into bales for shipping.

4 🧵

Spinning

Fibers are carded, drawn, and twisted into yarn. The yarn's thickness determines its end use.

5 🎨

Finishing

Fabric is woven or knitted, then bleached, dyed, and finished with mechanical or chemical treatments.

Maintenance

Care Instructions

Cotton is durable and easy to care for, but proper maintenance extends the life of your garments and reduces environmental impact.

🌡️

Washing

Machine wash warm (40°C) for whites, cool (30°C) for colors. Turn printed items inside out.

🧴

Detergent

Use mild, eco-friendly detergent. Avoid bleach — it weakens fibers and harms the environment.

🌀

Drying

Air dry when possible. Tumble dry low if using a machine. High heat causes shrinkage.

🔥

Ironing

Iron on high heat while slightly damp. Cotton can withstand high temperatures better than most fibers.

🗄️

Storage

Store in a cool, dry place. Cotton is not moth-prone but can mildew if stored in damp conditions.
